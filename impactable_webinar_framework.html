Impactable — Internal Framework

LinkedIn Live
Content System

A repeatable process for planning, running, and distributing LinkedIn Live sessions — from topic selection to post-event content output.

What this covers: Four phases of every webinar lifecycle — planning, pre-event, live execution, and post-event distribution. A starting point for building a repeatable process.
Phase 1

Planning

Topic selection
  • Identify themes relevant to ICP
  • Source ideas from SMEs and sales
  • Align with content calendar
  • Confirm angle and working title
Speaker lineup
  • Match topic to internal SME
  • Confirm availability and angle
  • Assign roles (host, guest, moderator)
Goals + KPIs
  • Set attendance target
  • Define lead capture goal
  • Identify content output targets
  • Agree on reporting format
Format + run of show
  • Decide structure (interview, panel, solo)
  • Draft agenda with timing
  • Plan Q+A window
  • Set CTA for the session
hands off to pre-event
Phase 2

Pre-event

Speaker prep
  • Send talking points brief
  • Share run of show with all guests
  • Q+A prep — anticipated questions
  • Schedule dry run if needed
Promo content
  • LinkedIn posts (Justin + company page)
  • Email to existing list
  • Teaser clips or quote graphics
  • Registration link live
Tech setup
  • Streaming platform set up and tested
  • Speaker audio and video confirmed
  • Recording enabled before going live
  • Moderator has chat access ready
Internal alignment
  • All speakers confirmed and briefed
  • Moderation role assigned
  • Promo posts scheduled
  • Day-of checklist distributed
hands off to live
Phase 3

Live

Pre-live check-in
  • All speakers online 10 min before
  • Sound, video, and stream confirmed
  • Run of show reviewed with everyone
  • Recording confirmed running
Moderator responsibilities
  • Manage chat and Q+A queue
  • Keep timing and agenda on track
  • Surface strong questions for speakers
  • Drop links and CTAs at right moments
hands off to post-event
Phase 4

Post-event

Recording distribution
  • Post replay on LinkedIn
  • Email to registrants + list
  • Upload to website or resource hub
Content repurposing
  • Pull 3–5 short clips for LinkedIn
  • Extract key quotes for posts
  • Turn insights into blog post or newsletter
  • Build carousel from session takeaways
Attendee follow-up
  • Thank you email within 24hrs
  • Include replay + resources
  • Soft CTA — next step or offer
Performance review
  • Attendance vs. target
  • Engagement rate and retention
  • Leads or pipeline attributed
  • Learnings for next session

Bringing in subject matter experts

Step 1
Build the SME roster
Map internal team members to topic areas. Know who can speak to what before you need them.
Step 2
Topic matching
When a topic is selected, identify the right SME — not just available, but genuinely knowledgeable and comfortable on camera.
Step 3
Pre-brief the SME
Share the angle, run of show, and their role at least one week out. They should know what they're there to add — not just show up.
Step 4
Capture their insight
Post-event, their perspective becomes standalone content — clips, quotes, or follow-up posts attributed to them.
